# Product Pitch Deck — Scaffold

**Audience:** Non-technical, business-focused
**Duration:** 4 minutes (~25–30 sec/slide average)
**Total slides:** 9
**Goal:** Audience leaves understanding *what the business does, why it matters, and why it will win.*

---

## Slide 1 — Title (~10 sec)

**Purpose:** Establish identity. Quiet, confident open.
**Flow role:** Sets the stage before the story begins.
**Contains:**
- Company name + logo
- Tagline (positioning, not a hook)
- Presenter name + role
- Date / context (optional)

---

## Slide 2 — The Hook (~20 sec)

**Purpose:** Grab attention with a single sharp idea — a stat, a question, a contrarian claim, or a vivid scenario.
**Flow role:** Earns the audience's full attention before any setup.
**Contains:**
- One bold statement, question, or image — nothing else
- No bullets, no logo clutter — pure focus

---

## Slide 3 — The Problem (~40 sec)

**Purpose:** Make the audience *feel* the pain. Anchor in a real customer, not abstract market language.
**Flow role:** Creates the tension the rest of the deck will resolve.
**Contains:**
- Who has the problem (customer archetype)
- What's broken today / what it costs them (time, money, frustration)
- Why existing solutions fall short — in plain terms

---

## Slide 4 — The Opportunity (~25 sec)

**Purpose:** Show this isn't a niche annoyance — it's a meaningful market.
**Flow role:** Transitions from "this hurts" to "this is worth solving."
**Contains:**
- Market size in everyday framing (e.g., "X million businesses spend $Y on this")
- Why now — the shift that makes this solvable today (behavior, regulation, tech)

---

## Slide 5 — The Solution (~45 sec)

**Purpose:** Reveal the product. Keep it concrete and visual.
**Flow role:** The "aha" moment — the audience should immediately picture using it.
**Contains:**
- One sentence: what the product is
- A visual (mockup, concept render, or before/after)
- The 3 things it will do for the customer — in their language, not ours

---

## Slide 6 — How It Works (~30 sec)

**Purpose:** Demystify without going technical. Show it's real and buildable.
**Flow role:** Builds credibility that the solution actually delivers.
**Contains:**
- 3-step flow: input → what happens → outcome
- A single sentence on the "secret sauce" (no jargon)

---

## Slide 7 — Why We'll Win (~40 sec)

**Purpose:** Replace traction with conviction. Since there's no usage data yet, lean on insight, team, and timing.
**Flow role:** The deck's persuasive moment — proves this isn't just an idea, it's the *right* idea from the *right* people *now*.
**Contains:**
- Unique insight: what we see that others don't
- Team credibility: why we're built to solve this (background, prior work, domain depth)
- Early validation signals (if any): customer interviews, waitlist, design partner conversations, LOIs
- 1–2 sentences on the moat we're building (data, distribution, IP, network effects)

---

## Slide 8 — Business Model & Roadmap (~30 sec)

**Purpose:** Answer "how do you make money?" and "what happens next?"
**Flow role:** Grounds the vision in commercial reality and a believable timeline.
**Contains:**
- How revenue will be earned (pricing concept, who pays, how often)
- Near-term milestones: beta → first customers → revenue
- What needs to be true in the next 6–12 months

---

## Slide 9 — The Ask & Vision (~30 sec)

**Purpose:** Tell them what you want and where this goes.
**Flow role:** Closes with momentum and a clear next step.
**Contains:**
- The vision in one sentence (what the world looks like if we win)
- The specific ask (investment, design partner, intro, pilot, hire)
- Contact info

---

## Pacing notes

- Slides 3, 5, and 7 are the load-bearing ones — spend more time here.
- Slides 2, 4, 6, 8 are connective tissue — keep them tight.
- Pre-traction, the persuasion shifts from "look at our numbers" to "look at our insight + team + timing." Slide 7 has to carry that weight.
- One image + one idea per slide reads best to a non-technical room.
